Why I'm writing this book

A coaching tool for authors

Every book starts from a reason. Before the first page, pause a moment to see what yours is. Six questions you can return to whenever the writing gets hard. There are no right or wrong answers, write what's true for you right now.

Your data stays on your device. Nothing is sent anywhere.

About this tool

It's not a test. It's a compass. No one grades your answers or compares them. They're only yours.

Most authors get stuck not from a lack of talent, but because they lose touch with the reason they started. When the "why" is clear, the "how" becomes much easier to carry.

How to use it: fill it in at the start, when you begin the book, and return to it whenever the writing gets hard or you start to doubt. The anchor from step 6 is the sentence you come back to, write it somewhere visible.

With a coach, it goes deeper: your answers open the conversation and become the thread that runs through it.

1

The spark

where it started
  • What made you start this book?
  • What idea or question won't leave you alone?
  • When did you first tell yourself "this is worth writing"?
2

The readers

who you're writing for
  • Who are the people who would love this book?
  • What do they seek in a book: an emotion, an escape, an idea, an answer?
  • Why would they open your book and not another?
3

The promise

what readers gain
  • What do readers understand, feel or do differently after they close the book?
  • What stays with them, even if they forget all the rest?
  • What, in essence, is your promise?
4

Why you, why now

what brought you here
  • What in your life has prepared you for this book?
  • Why is this the right moment in your life?
  • What do you have to say, the way no one else does?
5

If it stayed in the drawer

what would be lost
  • What's lost if you don't finish it?
  • Who is left without this book?
  • What do you feel when you imagine it still unwritten five years from now?
6

Your anchor

in one sentence

Capture everything you're thinking in a single sentence. This is what you return to when the writing gets hard.
