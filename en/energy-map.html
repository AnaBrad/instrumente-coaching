Energy Map

A coaching tool

About this exercise

Energy isn't shared out evenly. Some activities leave you full of life, others drain you without you noticing. The Energy Map makes visible what usually stays just a vague feeling at the end of the day.

How to work with it

  1. Look at the everyday activities in your week. Add or rename them so they fit your real life.
  2. For each one, choose a score from −5 (drains me) through 0 (neutral) up to +5 (charges me).
  3. Jot down a short thought wherever you feel the need: why it drains you, why it charges you.
  4. Look at the map. See which way the balance of your week is tipping.
  5. Save the map as a session. A month from now, compare.

What to look for in the result

  • What charges you — your sources of energy. Worth protecting, not leaving last on the list.
  • What drains you most — ask yourself whether it can be reduced, handed off, or seen differently. Not everything that drains you has to go, but it's worth seeing clearly.
  • The balance — a healthy week makes room for the things that charge you too, not only the ones that ask of you.
